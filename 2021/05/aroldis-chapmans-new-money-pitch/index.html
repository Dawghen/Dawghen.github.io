Aroldis Chapman's New Money Pitch

May 20, 2021 in MLB, RP

Aroldis Chapman, the elite closer of the New York Yankees is well-known as 100 mph fastball and a hard slider. He has been seeking his 3rd pitch type and finally seems to succeed in adding a new pitch type. He threw the splitters 3 times (out of 199 ) last season. From the animated pics, Chapman’s usage of the splitters are over 10% this season. (Currently, 13.3%)

This 100 mph closer is using the new pitch as a winning shot already. From the next table, Chapman’s splitter is right after Shohei Ohtani at pitch values per 100 pitch, aka wSF/C.

To catch up the idea of the pitch values, see the Fangraphs Glossary. In short, wSF is the total runs saved by a pitcher using that pitch, SF (split-finger fastball). Total run is calculated by the run expectation change by each pitch. The change of strike-ball-out counts change the run expectation which was calculated as an average for years.

Simply put, understand if wSF or wSF/C is high, then the pitch type is effective. Chapman’s wSF and wSF/C are higher than his wSL and wSL/C (Slider). Isn’t it surprising?
